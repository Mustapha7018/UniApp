About UG

The University of Ghana is a public university located in Accra, Ghana. It holds the distinction of being the oldest public university in the country. The university was established in 1948 as the University College of the Gold Coast, an affiliate college of the University of London, which supervised its academic programs and awarded degrees.

Located on the west side of the Accra Legon Hills and northeast of the center of Accra, the University of Ghana has over 60,000 registered students. It originally focused on liberal arts, social sciences, law, basic science, agriculture, and medicine. However, the curriculum has expanded to include more technology-based and vocational courses as well as postgraduate training.

Academic programs

The University of Ghana provides an extensive selection of courses across multiple fields of study. These encompass:

  • Arts
  • Sciences
  • Business
  • Law
  • Engineering
  • Social Sciences
  • Health Sciences

Additionally, the university offers a variety of advanced programs, including master’s and doctoral degrees in diverse areas.

General entry requirements

  1. WASSCE Holders: Must have credit passes in six subjects, including English, Mathematics, and three electives relevant to the chosen program.
  2. GCE A-Level Holders: Need passes in three subjects, one at least at Grade D or better, plus credit passes in five O-Level subjects including English and Mathematics.
  3. International Baccalaureate (IB) Holders: Require a minimum of 24 points with core and elective subjects.
  4. American High School Grade 12 Holders: Should have a final GPA of at least 3.0.
  5. Applicants with Other Qualifications: Considered on a case-by-case basis.
  6. Mature Students: Assessed based on age, experience, and performance in an entrance exam and/or interview.
  7. Postgraduate Applicants: Generally need a good first degree, at least a second-class lower division.
